# End Brick Template

Videos never end - they set up the next problem and point to the solution.

---

## Transition from Final Point

Bridge from your last content brick to the new problem.

**Do NOT:**
- Summarize what they learned
- Say "that's it" or "that's a wrap"
- Use generic closing language

**Your transition:**
> [Bridge sentence that flows naturally from your final point]

---

## New Problem to Poke

**Identify a related problem:**

What challenge might viewers face AFTER implementing what you taught?

**Brainstorm:**
- After they do [what you taught], they might struggle with ___
- The next logical challenge is ___
- If they don't also fix ___, everything I taught won't work

**Your new problem:**
> [The related problem viewers will face]

---

## Stakes (Why It Matters)

Make clear why this new problem is important.

**Template:**
> "The thing is, now you know how to [what you taught], you've got to make sure [the new problem], because if you don't, [consequence]."

**Your stakes:**
> [Why solving this new problem matters]

---

## Specific Next Video

**Do NOT:** Send to generic channel page or "watch more"

**Identify the specific video:**

**Video Title:** [Exact title of the video you're pointing to]

**What They'll Learn:** [What problem this video solves]

**CTA Template:**
> "So watch this video next and I'll show you [what they'll learn that solves the new problem]."

**Your CTA:**
> [Your specific call to action]

---

## Complete End Brick

**Assemble all pieces:**

> [Transition from final point]
>
> [New problem with stakes]
>
> [Specific next video CTA]

## End Screen Planning

**End screen element:** Will appear over your CTA

## Example End Brick

> "The thing is, now you know how to reduce the rash, you've got to make sure you stop getting it in the first place because it will come back. So watch this video next and I'll teach you how to do just that."

**Analysis:**
- Transition: "The thing is, now you know how to reduce the rash"
- New problem: "you've got to make sure you stop getting it in the first place"
- Stakes: "because it will come back"
- CTA: "So watch this video next and I'll teach you how to do just that"

---

## End Brick Checklist

- [ ] No summary of video content
- [ ] No "that's it" or closing language
- [ ] New problem is RELATED to current video topic
- [ ] Stakes are clear (why this matters)
- [ ] Points to SPECIFIC video, not generic channel
- [ ] CTA uses "watch this video next" language
- [ ] End screen element placement planned

**Business Impact:**
Proper end bricks can increase end screen CTR from ~1% to 20-25%.
