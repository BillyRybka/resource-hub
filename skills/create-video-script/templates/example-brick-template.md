## Option 1: Story

### Story Type Selection

| Type | Rating | Best When |
|------|--------|-----------|
| [ ] Personal Experience | 10/10 | You overcame this challenge yourself |
| [ ] Client/Student Success | 10/10 | You helped someone else succeed |
| [ ] Third-Party | 8/10 | Unique example (avoid overused ones) |
| [ ] Fictional | 7/10 | Only if you can execute well |

### Story Structure

**Beginning:**
- **Who:** [Name - real or specific]
- **Problem:** [The problem they faced]
- **Why:** [Why solving this was crucial - creates stakes]

> [One sentence that establishes Who + Problem + Why]

**Middle:**
- **Conflict/Obstacle:** [What got in the way?]
- **How Overcome:** [This is where the lesson lives]

> [The journey - keep only details that impact the ending]

**End:**
- **Resolution:** [Did they get what they wanted? Needed? Both? Neither?]
- **Lesson:** [What this proves about your point]

> [The payoff]

### Story Checklist
- [ ] Real person or specific composite
- [ ] Specific numbers/timeframes
- [ ] Clear connection to the point
- [ ] Relatable situation
- [ ] Concrete consequence
- [ ] Comes BEFORE explaining the point (not after)
- [ ] No unnecessary details

## Option 2: Metaphor

### 5-Step Creation Process

**Step 1: Define the Problem**
- What is the problem? [Answer]
- How does it make people feel? [Answer]
- Characteristic features: [List]

**Step 2: Define the Solution**
- What is the solution? [Answer]
- What feelings/outcomes does it provide? [Answer]
- Steps or characteristics: [List]

**Step 3: Identify Core Analogy**
- Problem is like: [Something with similar characteristics]
- Solution is like: [Something that embodies the outcome]

**Step 4: Create the Metaphor**
> "[X] is [Y]..."
> [Complete metaphorical statement]

**Step 5: Test and Refine**
- [ ] Makes sense when said aloud
- [ ] Relatable to your audience
- [ ] Not forced or unclear

### Physical Prop (Optional but Powerful)
**Prop:** [What physical object could demonstrate this?]
**How to use it:** [Description of visual demonstration]

### Metaphor Checklist
- [ ] Uses familiar, universally understood concept
- [ ] Specific but not overly complex
- [ ] Works as "X IS Y" not just "X is LIKE Y"
- [ ] Has clear transition in and out
