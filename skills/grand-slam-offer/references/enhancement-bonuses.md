# Bonuses - Value Stacking Mastery

Bonuses expand the price-to-value discrepancy and get people to buy who otherwise wouldn't.

## The Core Principle

> "A single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into its component parts and stacked as bonuses."

**Why this works:**
- Each bonus expands perceived value
- Subconsciously communicates core offer must be valuable (bonuses are "extras")
- Addresses specific objections one at a time
- Creates "how can I NOT buy this?" moment

This is why every infomercial says "But wait... there's more!"

---

## The 10 Bonus Bullets

### 1. Always Offer Bonuses

Never present a core offer without bonuses. Even if you were going to include these things anyway, separate them out and present them as added value.

### 2. Give Each Bonus a Special Name with Benefit

**Weak:** "Video training module"
**Strong:** "The 20-Minute Grocery Blitz System"

**Weak:** "Checklist"
**Strong:** "The Never-Miss-A-Step Implementation Checklist"

### 3. Tell the Complete Story

For each bonus, cover:

| Element | What to Include |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Relation** | How it connects to their problem |
| **What** | What it actually is |
| **Origin** | How you discovered/created it |
| **Improvement** | How it will make their life better |

**Example:**
> "One thing I noticed working with clients is they always struggled with eating out (relation). So I created the 'Live It Up While Slimming Down' Eating Out Guide (what). After testing with 500+ clients, I documented exactly which items to order at 47 popular restaurants (origin). Now you can enjoy dinners with friends without worrying about derailing your progress (improvement)."

### 4. Provide Proof

Each bonus should have credibility:
- Stat: "Used by 10,000+ customers"
- Past client: "Sarah used this to lose 12 lbs in her first month"
- Personal: "I used this exact system when I lost 50 lbs"

### 5. Paint a Vivid Mental Image

Describe life AFTER using the bonus:

> "Imagine walking into any restaurant, confidently ordering exactly what you want, and waking up the next day lighter than the day before. That's what this guide makes possible."

### 6. Assign a Price Tag and Justify It

Every bonus needs a dollar value with reasoning:

**Justification methods:**
- Time value: "Saves 10 hours = $500 at $50/hour"
- Money saved: "Saves $200/month on groceries = $2,400/year"
- Alternative cost: "Personal nutritionist charges $300/session Ã— 10 = $3,000"
- Results value: "Gets you results 3 weeks faster = worth $1,000"

### 7. Tools & Checklists > Trainings

**Higher value perception:**
- Checklists
- Templates
- Swipe files
- Calculators
- Scripts

**Lower value perception:**
- More training videos
- Additional modules
- Extra content

**Why:** Tools require less effort and time (bottom of Value Equation).

### 8. Address Specific Obstacles

Each bonus should solve a specific objection:

| Objection | Bonus That Solves It |
|-----------|---------------------|
| "I travel a lot" | Travel Workout & Eating Guide |
| "I eat out often" | Restaurant Survival Guide |
| "My family won't eat this" | Family-Friendly Meal Conversion Kit |
| "I don't have time" | 5-Minute Meal System |
| "I'll forget what to do" | Daily Action Checklist |

### 9. Solve Their NEXT Problem

Think ahead: What will they need after they start?

**Course about getting clients:**
- Bonus: Client Onboarding System
- Bonus: Retention Scripts
- Bonus: Upsell Templates

**Weight loss program:**
- Bonus: Maintenance Phase Guide
- Bonus: Wardrobe Transition Tips
- Bonus: Progress Photo Guide

### 10. Total Bonus Value Should Eclipse Core Offer

If your core offer is $997, bonuses should total $3,000-$10,000.

**This creates the "snap":** The price-to-value discrepancy becomes so large it breaks their resistance.

## Presenting Bonuses: 1-on-1 vs. Group

### One-on-One Sales

**Sequence:**
1. Present core offer
2. Ask for the sale
3. If YES â†’ Reveal bonuses as surprise ("You're also going to get...")
4. If NO â†’ Present bonus that matches their objection â†’ Ask again
5. Repeat with additional bonuses as needed

**Script for handling objection:**
> "I totally understand. You know what, because [their objection], I want to add something. [Present relevant bonus]. Does that feel fair enough?"

**Why it works:** People have a hard time rejecting reciprocity. You're adding value to accommodate their concern.

### Group/Webinar Sales

**Sequence:**
1. Present core offer with value
2. Stack each bonus one at a time
3. Build running total
4. Show massive discrepancy at end
5. Add urgency/scarcity to bonuses

## Adding Scarcity & Urgency to Bonuses

Bonuses become MORE powerful with scarcity/urgency.

### Bonuses with Scarcity

**Version 1 - Exclusive access:**
> "Only people who sign up for [Program] will have access to [Bonus] - it's never for sale anywhere else."

**Version 2 - Limited quantity:**
> "I have 3 tickets left to my $5,000 live event. Sign up today and one is yours as a bonus."

### Bonuses with Urgency

**Version 1 - Time-limited:**
> "If you enroll today, I'll include [Bonus] valued at $1,000. This bonus expires at midnight."

**Version 2 - Rewarding action:**
> "I add this bonus for action-takers only. Enroll in the next 24 hours and it's yours."

## Partner/Affiliate Bonuses (Advanced)

Get other businesses to provide bonuses for FREE in exchange for exposure to your customers.

### How It Works

1. Identify businesses that serve your customers' NEXT need
2. Negotiate free products/services as bonuses
3. They get exposure to qualified prospects (your customers)
4. You get high-value bonuses at zero cost
5. Optional: Negotiate affiliate commission for referrals

### Example: Pain Clinic Offer

**Partner bonuses secured:**
- 2 free massage sessions from local therapist ($100 value)
- 2 free chiropractic adjustments ($100 value)
- Discount on anti-inflammatory foods ($50 savings)
- Discounts on braces/orthotics ($150 savings)
- Free personal training session + 1 month pool access ($100 value)
- Pharmacy discounts on relevant medications ($100/month savings)

**Total partner bonus value:** $600+ (covers your entire price if offer is $400)

### The Jedi Move

Negotiate BOTH:
1. Group discount for your customers
2. Commission to you for referrals

**Example (Supplement company):**
- Customers get 30% discount (they love you)
- You get 40% commission on sales (you make money)
- Partner gets customers (they win)

**Everyone wins.**

### Partner Bonus Script

> "I have [X] customers who will need [your service] after working with me. I'd like to include [your product/service] as a bonus in my offer. In exchange, you'll get exposure to highly qualified prospects. Would you be open to providing [specific offer] for my customers?"

## What Should Be a Bonus vs. Core Offer?

### Make It a Bonus When:

- It's short but high-quality (would get lost in main content)
- It can almost stand on its own
- It addresses a specific subset of customers
- It has high perceived value but low time investment
- It's a tool/template/checklist (condenses information)
- You want to highlight it specifically

### Keep It in Core Offer When:

- It's essential to the main transformation
- It requires significant time investment
- It builds on previous content sequentially
- Removing it would hurt results

### The Wow Factor Test

> "Would this make someone say 'wow, I didn't expect that' if they discovered it after purchasing?"

If yes â†’ Make it a bonus
If no â†’ Keep in core offer

## Pro Tips

### Never Discount - Add Bonuses Instead

> "When trying to close a deal, never discount the main offer. It teaches customers your prices are negotiable. Adding bonuses to increase value is far superiorâ€”it puts you in a position of strength and goodwill rather than weakness."

### The Bonus Ladder

When handling objections, don't reveal all bonuses at once:

1. First objection â†’ Bonus 1
2. Second objection â†’ Bonus 2
3. Third objection â†’ Bonus 3

Each feels like a personal accommodation. They feel obligated to say yes.

### Information Products as Bonuses

Perfect because:
- High perceived value
- Zero marginal cost
- No operational effort (just add login)
- Can be created once, used forever

### Event Tickets as Bonuses

Perfect because:
- High perceived value
- Fixed cost regardless of attendees
- Creates urgency (date-bound)
- Opportunity to upsell at event

## Summary

1. **Always use bonuses** - Never present naked offers
2. **Name them powerfully** - Benefits in the title
3. **Tell the story** - Relation, what, origin, improvement
4. **Prove value** - Stats, testimonials, comparisons
5. **Paint the picture** - Life after using it
6. **Assign value** - With clear justification
7. **Tools > Training** - Lower effort = higher value
8. **Solve objections** - Each bonus handles specific concern
9. **Think ahead** - Solve their next problem
10. **Eclipse core** - Total bonus value > offer price

Build your bonus vault. Pull from it strategically. Watch conversions soar.
