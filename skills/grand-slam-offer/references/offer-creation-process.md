## Step 2: List All Problems

### The Principle

Every obstacle between your prospect and their dream outcome is an opportunity to create value. More problems identified = more value created = higher prices justified.

### The Four Problem Categories (Value Equation Aligned)

For EACH action they must take, apply these four lenses:

| Value Driver | Their Appraisal | What They're Thinking |
|--------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Dream Outcome** | "Not worth it" | "This will not be financially worth it" |
| **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** | "Won't work for me" | "It won't work for me specifically. I won't be able to stick with it. External factors will get in my way." |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | "Too hard" | "This will be too hard, confusing. I won't like it. I will suck at it." |
| **Time Delay** | "Takes too long" | "This will take too much time to do. I am too busy. It will take too long to work. It won't be convenient for me." |

**Note on Objections:** Objections about buying or using your solution ("What if it doesn't work for me?" / "I don't have time to learn this" / "My situation is different") are Perceived Likelihood and Effort & Sacrifice problems. Treat them like any other problemâ€”they need to be solved somewhere in your offer.

**For each category, ask:**
- "Not worth it" â†’ How can I make this MORE financially worth it?
- "Won't work for me" â†’ How can I prove this WILL work for them specifically?
- "Too hard" â†’ How can I make it easy? Simple? Enjoyable?
- "Takes too long" â†’ How can I make it faster? More convenient?

### The "Next Need" Hack (Before/During/After)

Problems exist at every stage of the customer journey. Don't just solve problems DURINGâ€”solve them BEFORE they start and AFTER they finish:

| Phase | What to Ask | Example Problems |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **BEFORE** (they haven't started) | "What stops them from beginning?" | Setup, preparation, clearing schedule, getting buy-in |
| **DURING** (they're in the program) | "What stops them from completing?" | Confusion, motivation, competing priorities, setbacks |
| **AFTER** (the program ends) | "What stops them from maintaining?" | Backsliding, new situations, social pressure, travel |

**Why this matters:**
- BEFORE problems = reasons they don't START (lost sales)
- DURING problems = reasons they don't COMPLETE (poor results)
- AFTER problems = reasons they don't MAINTAIN (no referrals)

## Step 3: Turn Problems Into Solutions

### The Principle

Every problem statement converts directly into a solution statement. This is copywriting 101â€”address their exact fear with your exact solution.

### Conversion Formula

**Problem:** [Negative statement about obstacle]
**Solution:** "How to [reverse the negative] (even if [their excuse])"

### Conversion Examples

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| "I don't know what's actually healthy" | "How to know exactly what to buy (no nutrition degree required)" |
| "Grocery shopping takes forever" | "How to complete healthy shopping in under 20 minutes" |
| "I'm a terrible cook" | "How anyone can make delicious meals (even if you burn toast)" |
| "Cooking takes too long" | "How to cook healthy meals in under 5 minutes" |
| "I always fall off diets" | "How to stay consistent (even when life gets crazy)" |
| "I hate the gym" | "How to get better results than gym-goers without a gym" |
| "I'll miss happy hour" | "How to socialize without derailing your progress" |

### Solution Naming

Each solution should become a named component of your offer:

**Solution:** "How to complete healthy shopping in under 20 minutes"
**Named Component:** "The 20-Minute Grocery Blitz System"

**Solution:** "How to cook healthy meals in under 5 minutes"
**Named Component:** "Ready in 5 Busy Parent Cooking Guide"

## Step 5: Trim & Stack

### The Principle

Not everything you CAN deliver SHOULD be delivered. Remove low-value items, keep high-value items, and stack them for maximum perceived value.

### The Trim Matrix

| | Low Cost | High Cost |

### The 80% Gross Margin Rule (Service Businesses)

For service businesses, target 80%+ gross margins. If you charge $100, fulfillment should cost $20 or less.

**Why 80%?**

| Category | Approximate % |
|----------|---------------|
| Cost of Fulfillment | ~20% |
| Cost of Acquisition (marketing, sales, commissions) | ~20-30% |
| Infrastructure (software, admin, billing) | ~15-20% |
| **Net Profit** | **~30%+** |

You should be most profitable in the beginningâ€”margins only decrease as you add infrastructure and team.

### Evaluation Criteria

For each delivery vehicle, ask:

1. **Dream Outcome:** Does this directly advance their result?
2. **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement:** Does this increase their belief it will work for THEM?
3. **Time Delay:** Does this speed up their results?
4. **Effort & Sacrifice:** Does this make their life easier?

If the answer isn't a clear "yes" to at least one, remove it.

### Trim Criteria Refined

**Limit high-cost items:** Keep 1-2 high-cost delivery items MAX per problem area. Fill the rest with high-value, low-cost items.

**The "One Fell Swoop" test:** Some delivery methods solve multiple problems at once. A personal grocery tour solves "confusing," "takes too long," AND "won't like it" simultaneously. Prioritize these multi-problem solvers.

**The balance:**
- Low-cost items handle the HOW (recordings, tools, templates, guides)
- High-cost items handle the FEAR (personal review, sign-off, done-for-you elements)

## The Sales-Fulfillment Continuum

### The Principle

There's always a tradeoff between ease of selling and ease of fulfillment.

```
Easy to Sell â—„â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â–º Easy to Fulfill
(Do everything)                       (Do nothing)
```

### Strategy by Stage

**Starting out:** Over-deliver like crazy. Do more than you'll eventually do.
- Build case studies
- Learn what actually works
- Generate word-of-mouth

**Scaling:** Optimize for efficiency without sacrificing results.
- Systematize what works
- Create one-to-many solutions
- Reduce high-cost delivery

### Hormozi's Evolution

**Phase 1:** Flew to gyms, lived there 21 days, did everything personally
**Phase 2:** Taught gym owners to do it themselves remotely
**Result:** Same promise, 1/3 the effort, 100x the scale

### The Mantra

> "Create flow. Monetize flow. Then add friction."

1. Generate demand first (prove people want it)
2. Get them to say yes (over-deliver if needed)
3. Optimize operations (only after cash flows)

## The Tesla Pyramid: Build Top-Down

Build your business from the TOP down, not bottom up.

**The Tesla example:** They started with the $250k Roadster (few customers, highest price), then the $100k Model S/X, then finally the $30k Model 3/Y for mass market. They didn't start cheap and try to work upâ€”they started premium and expanded down.

**Why top-down works:**
- **Cash flow first** â€” High ticket + fewer clients = faster profitability
- **Price anchoring** â€” Your mid-tier feels cheap compared to your top-tier
- **Proof flows downward** â€” Results from high-touch validate lower-touch offers
- **No premature scaling** â€” Don't solve problems you don't have yet

**Applied to services:** Start with custom 1-on-1 / Done-For-You (learn, get testimonials, refine). Then create group / Done-With-You (scale proof, build systems). Finally, course / DIY (leverage, reach more people).

**The mistake:** "I need to be scalable first." You don't need scalabilityâ€”you need clients. Get clients, then figure out scale.

## Troubleshooting

### "I can't think of enough problems"

- Interview 10 customers/prospects
- Read Amazon reviews of competing products
- Search forums/Reddit for complaints
- Ask: "What did you try before that didn't work?"

### "My solutions seem generic"

- Add specificity: "5-minute" instead of "quick"
- Add proof: "used by 10,000+ customers"
- Add mechanism: "using the XYZ method"
- Add avatar: "even if you're a busy mom"

### "I can't justify high values"

- Calculate time saved Ã— hourly rate
- Calculate money saved over time
- Compare to alternative solutions
- Factor in cost of NOT solving

### "My offer seems too complicated"

- Group related items into bundles
- Name bundles, not individual items
- Focus on 3-5 major components
- Move details to supporting materials
